# Startup Funding Guide for Philippines

## Overview

This guide will help you find funding for Tara at different stages of development.

---

## Funding Stages

### Stage 1: Pre-Seed / Bootstrapping (₱0 - ₱2M)
**When:** Idea stage, building MVP
**What you need:** Enough to build and launch

### Stage 2: Seed Round (₱2M - ₱20M or $50K - $500K)
**When:** MVP launched, early traction
**What you need:** Grow user base, hire team

### Stage 3: Series A (₱20M - ₱100M or $500K - $2M)
**When:** Product-market fit, scaling
**What you need:** Scale operations, marketing

## Funding Sources in the Philippines

### 1. Bootstrapping (Self-Funding)
**Amount:** Whatever you can afford
**Pros:** Full control, no dilution
**Cons:** Limited resources, slower growth

**How to maximize:**
- Keep day job while building
- Freelance for extra income
- Pre-sell services to early customers
- Start small, reinvest profits

---

### 2. Friends & Family
**Amount:** ₱100K - ₱2M
**Pros:** Easy to access, flexible terms
**Cons:** Can strain relationships

**Tips:**
- Be professional (written agreement)
- Clear expectations
- Realistic about risks
- Set repayment terms

### 3. Government Programs

#### DOST (Department of Science and Technology)
**Program:** SETUP (Small Enterprise Technology Upgrading Program)
- **Amount:** Up to ₱1M
- **Type:** Grant (non-equity, refundable)
- **Website:** https://setup.dost.gov.ph
- **Requirements:** Registered business, Filipino-owned, 10-199 employees

**How to apply:**
1. Register your business (DTI/SEC)
2. Prepare business plan
3. Visit DOST regional office
4. Submit proposal
5. Present to evaluation committee

#### DTI (Department of Trade and Industry)
**Program:** Pondo sa Pagbabago at Pag-asenso (P3)
- **Amount:** ₱200K
- **Type:** Loan (0% interest for 3 years)
- **Website:** https://www.dti.gov.ph
- **For:** Micro and small enterprises

#### DICT (Department of Information and Communications Technology)
**Program:** Startup Grant Fund
- **Amount:** Varies
- **Type:** Grant
- **Focus:** ICT startups
- **Website:** https://dict.gov.ph

## Funding Strategy for Tara

### Phase 1: Pre-Seed (MVP Development)
**Timeline:** Months 1-6
**Goal:** Build and launch MVP
**Funding needed:** ₱500K - ₱2M

**Recommended sources:**
1. **Bootstrap** (₱100K-500K)
   - Your savings
   - Freelance work
   - Side projects

2. **Friends & Family** (₱200K-500K)
   - Close connections who believe in you

3. **IdeaSpace or QBO** (₱500K-1.5M equity-free)
   - Apply immediately
   - Strong Philippine focus is an advantage

**Milestones to hit:**
- Working MVP
- 100-500 registered users
- 10-50 active suppliers
- 5-10 content creators
- 10+ bookings completed

---

### Phase 2: Seed Round
**Timeline:** Months 7-12
**Goal:** Grow to 10K users, prove product-market fit
**Funding needed:** ₱5M - ₱20M ($100K - $400K)

**Recommended sources:**
1. **Launchgarage or Plug and Play** (₱500K-2M + mentorship)
   - 3-4 month programs
   - Get you ready for investors

2. **Angel Investors** (₱3M-10M)
   - Kaya Founders
   - Individual angels
   - Aim for 3-5 angels

3. **Kickstart Ventures** (₱5M-15M)
   - Strong track record in Philippines
   - Good for travel/marketplace

**Milestones to hit:**
- 10,000+ users
- 500+ suppliers
- 100+ active creators
- ₱1M+ GMV (Gross Merchandise Value)
- Month-over-month growth: 20%+

---

### Phase 3: Series A
**Timeline:** Year 2-3
**Goal:** Scale nationwide, become market leader
**Funding needed:** ₱50M - ₱200M ($1M - $4M)

**Recommended sources:**
1. **Regional VCs**
   - Openspace Ventures
   - Golden Gate Ventures
   - Gobi Partners

2. **Strategic investors**
   - Ayala (tourism connections)
   - Globe/PLDT (digital services)

**Milestones to hit before Series A:**
- 100,000+ users
- 2,000+ suppliers nationwide
- ₱50M+ GMV
- Profitable unit economics
- Clear path to profitability

## How to Prepare for Fundraising

### 1. Build a Strong Pitch Deck (10-15 slides)

**Slide 1: Problem**
- Filipinos struggle to plan trips (fragmented tools)
- Content creators can't monetize easily
- Boop doesn't serve Philippines market

**Slide 2: Solution**
- Tara: All-in-one travel platform
- Free trip planner + social content + booking
- Built for Philippines market

**Slide 3: Market Opportunity**
- Philippines travel market: $6.7B (2025)
- 110M population, 50% under 30
- Growing middle class, rising domestic travel

**Slide 4: Product**
- Screenshots of app
- Key features
- User flow

**Slide 5: Business Model**
- Commission on bookings (10-15%)
- Creator affiliates (5-7%)
- Supplier subscriptions (₱999-2,999/month)

**Slide 6: Traction**
- Users, suppliers, GMV
- Growth charts
- Key metrics

**Slide 7: Competition**
- Boop, Booking.com, Instagram, Klook
- How you're different

**Slide 8: Go-to-Market Strategy**
- How you'll acquire users
- Marketing channels
- Partnerships

**Slide 9: Team**
- Your background
- Key team members
- Advisors

**Slide 10: Financials**
- Revenue projections (3 years)
- Key assumptions
- Path to profitability

**Slide 11: The Ask**
- How much you're raising
- What you'll use it for
- Milestones you'll hit

### 2. Financial Projections

**Create a simple model:**
- Users (Month 1-36)
- Conversion rate (% who book)
- Average booking value
- Commission rate
- Revenue = Users × Conversion × Booking Value × Commission
- Costs (team, servers, marketing)

**Example projections for Year 1:**
- Month 1: 100 users → ₱10K revenue
- Month 6: 2,000 users → ₱300K revenue
- Month 12: 10,000 users → ₱1.5M revenue

### 3. Build Credibility

**Before raising:**
- Launch MVP
- Get initial users (even if small)
- Get testimonials
- Press coverage (tech blogs, local media)
- Win startup competitions
- Build advisor network

## Investor Meeting Tips

### Preparation
1. **Know your numbers** cold
   - User growth rate
   - GMV
   - Burn rate
   - Runway

2. **Practice your pitch**
   - 3-minute version
   - 10-minute version
   - 30-minute version with Q&A

3. **Anticipate questions**
   - "How do you compete with Boop?"
   - "What's your customer acquisition cost?"
   - "Why Philippines first, not SEA?"
   - "What if Booking.com copies you?"

### During Meeting
1. **Tell a story** - Make it personal, compelling
2. **Show passion** - Investors invest in founders, not just ideas
3. **Be honest** - Don't bullshit, admit what you don't know
4. **Ask for feedback** - Even if they don't invest
5. **Follow up** - Thank you email within 24 hours

### Red Flags to Avoid
- ❌ "We have no competition"
- ❌ Unrealistic projections
- ❌ No clear go-to-market strategy
- ❌ Not knowing your metrics
- ❌ Bad-mouthing competitors
- ❌ Unclear use of funds

## Valuation Guidelines

### Pre-Seed / Idea Stage
**Typical valuation:** ₱10M - ₱50M ($200K - $1M)
**Raise:** ₱1M - ₱5M for 10-20% equity

### Seed Stage (MVP + Traction)
**Typical valuation:** ₱50M - ₱200M ($1M - $4M)
**Raise:** ₱5M - ₱20M for 10-15% equity

### Series A (Scaling)
**Typical valuation:** ₱200M - ₱1B ($4M - $20M)
**Raise:** ₱50M - ₱200M for 15-25% equity

**Factors that increase valuation:**
- Strong traction (users, revenue)
- High growth rate
- Experienced team
- Large market size
- Competitive moat

## Final Tips

1. **Start fundraising before you need money**
   - Takes 3-6 months typically
   - Don't wait until you run out

2. **Focus on product first**
   - Traction > Pitch deck
   - Build something people want

3. **Network constantly**
   - Attend events
   - Be visible
   - Help other founders

4. **Don't give up too much equity early**
   - Keep at least 60-70% after seed round
   - You need room for Series A, B, etc.

5. **Choose investors carefully**
   - It's a long-term relationship
   - Smart money > Dumb money
   - Get investors who add value

6. **Be patient**
   - Fundraising is hard
   - Rejection is normal
   - Keep building
